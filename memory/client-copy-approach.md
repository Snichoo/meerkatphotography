The Meerkat Photography client is ESL and not a strong writer; her notes are vague ideas, not literal copy to transcribe.

**Why:** Sam (the dev) explicitly said to take her instructions as rough direction and write something much better from scratch, replacing existing text rather than anchoring to her exact wording or the prior site copy.

**How to apply:** When she sends feedback, separate the concrete UI asks (which are literal) from the copy direction (which is a loose brief). Write professional, polished copy in the agreed brand voice. Established positioning: **one friendly, versatile Melbourne photographer  Kit  covering both celebrations (weddings, families, birthdays, pets) AND business work (headshots, products, events).** Voice is warm but professional, first-person singular ("I"), CTA is "Get a quote". Avoid the old B2B-only framing (commercial/corporate, "brand-aligned imagery", required business-name fields). See [[meerkat-site-structure]].
